About Swami Vivekananda

Swami Vivekananda was a great Indian monk and spiritual leader. He introduced Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the western world. He is best known for his speech at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago in 1893.

Major Contributions

  • Represented India at the World Parliament of Religions in Chicago (1893)
  • Founder of Ramakrishna Mission
  • Spread teachings of Vedanta and Yoga
  • Inspired youth towards nation building

Famous Quote

"Arise, awake and stop not until the goal is reached."
